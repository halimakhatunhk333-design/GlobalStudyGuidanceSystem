Rules for Funding

Grades Matter

You must keep a 3.0 GPA (or 60%+) to keep your scholarship or loan active.

Full-Time Study

You must take a full course load (usually 4 subjects) to stay legal.

Working Hours

Most countries only let you work 20 hours a week during class time.

Stay in Major

Changing your subject (e.g. Science to Arts) may cancel your funding.

Annual Review

You must submit your tax papers and results every year to renew the fund.

Clean Record

Any illegal activity or breaking university rules will stop your financial aid.

Fraud Warning

Real universities only email you from addresses ending in .edu or .ac.uk. Never pay agents cash for "guaranteed" scholarships.

Student File Requirements

To open a student file at a bank and send money abroad, you need these documents:

Personal Documents

  • Valid Passport (Original and Color Copy).
  • Student Visa or Entry Permit.
  • National ID Card (NID).

Educational Documents

  • Unconditional Offer Letter.
  • I-20 Form (USA) or CAS Letter (UK).
  • Original Certificates and Transcripts.

Financial Documents

  • Sponsor's ID and 3 years Income Tax records.
  • Bank Statement (last 6 months).
  • Affidavit of Support.

Loan Eligibility Check

Most Fintech lenders (like MPOWER or Prodigy) look for these things:

Academic Standing

  • Within 2 years of finishing degree.
  • Minimum GPA 3.0.
  • Admission to Top Ranked university.

Course Eligibility

  • STEM Programs.
  • MBA or Healthcare Management.
  • Law or Public Policy.
