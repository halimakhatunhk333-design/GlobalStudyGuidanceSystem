Career Success & Job Rights

From day one of your studies to your first full-time job. Here is the exact path to employment in your destination country.

01. Employment Strategy
Early Stage

The First Semester Plan

You must start preparing for a job the moment you land. Jobs abroad are not just about degrees; they are about connections.

  • LinkedIn Profile: Connect with seniors (alumni) who already have jobs in your field.
  • Local Resume: Convert your CV to the specific local format (e.g., Canadian Resume vs. US Resume).
  • Job Fairs: Every university holds 2 major fairs. You must attend these to meet recruiters in person.
Market Secret

The Hidden Job Market

Over 70% of high-paying jobs are never posted on public websites. They are filled through "Referrals."

  • How it works: A current employee recommends you to the manager. You get an interview immediately.
  • Strategy: Ask seniors for "Informational Interviews" to learn about their company.
  • Referral Power: A referred candidate is 10x more likely to be hired than a random online applicant.

03. Critical Action Steps

Work Identification (ID)

To work legally (even part-time), you need a tax ID. Apply for these on your first week.

  • USA: Social Security Number (SSN).
  • UK: National Insurance Number (NINO).
  • Canada: Social Insurance Number (SIN).

Part-Time Jobs

Most students work in retail, tutoring, or admin to cover their food and rent costs.

  • On-Campus: Working for the university (library, cafe). Best for your CV.
  • Off-Campus: Supermarkets, logistics, or delivery. Easier to find.
  • Pay: Expect $15–$25 per hour depending on the country.
